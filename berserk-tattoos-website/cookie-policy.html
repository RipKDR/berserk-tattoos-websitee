Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Berserk Tattoos ("we", "our", "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website berserktattoos.com.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies help websites remember information about your visit, such as your preferences, login status, or items in a shopping cart.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: Cookies that remain on your device until they expire or you delete them
  • First-party cookies: Set by the website you're visiting (berserktattoos.com)
  • Third-party cookies: Set by other services we use (Google Analytics, Stripe)

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential functionality: To save your booking progress so you don't lose your information
  • Analytics: To understand how visitors use our website and improve our services
  • Performance: To monitor website performance and detect technical issues
  • Payment processing: To enable secure payment transactions through Stripe

4. Local Storage

In addition to cookies, we use localStorage, a browser technology that stores data on your device:

What is localStorage?

localStorage is similar to cookies but can store more data and doesn't automatically send data to our servers with every request. It persists until you clear your browser data.

How we use localStorage:

  • Booking form data: We save your booking form inputs (name, email, phone, tattoo details) so you don't lose your progress if you accidentally close the browser or refresh the page
  • User preferences: May store UI preferences like collapsed/expanded sections

What's stored in localStorage:

  • Your name, email, phone number (only while actively booking)
  • Selected artist
  • Tattoo details (placement, size, description)
  • Appointment date and time selection

Security: localStorage data remains on your device and is only used to restore your booking form if needed. You can clear this data at any time through your browser settings.

5. How to Control Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Here's how for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and site permissions

Most browsers allow you to:

  • See what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies from specific websites
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser
  • Set exceptions for websites you trust

5.2 Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can opt-out of Google Analytics tracking by:

  • Installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Enabling "Do Not Track" in your browser settings
  • Using privacy-focused browser extensions like uBlock Origin or Privacy Badger

5.3 Impact of Blocking Cookies

Important: What Happens if You Block Cookies?

If you block or delete cookies:

  • Essential cookies: The booking system may not work properly, and your form data won't be saved
  • Analytics cookies: No impact on website functionality, but we won't be able to improve the user experience based on visitor behavior
  • Payment cookies: Stripe payments may not work correctly

We recommend allowing at least essential and payment cookies for the best experience.
