Amelia Kelso

Fine Line Specialist

Amelia's journey into fine line tattooing began with classical art training and an obsession with botanical illustration. Over 8 years, she's refined a signature approach that prioritizes clean lines, thoughtful composition, and designs that age gracefully. Every consultation starts with understanding your vision, your skin, and how the tattoo will live with you for decades.

Her portfolio spans delicate wrist florals to complex full-back pieces, but the thread is consistent: precision that borders on obsessive, patience that ensures perfection, and an artistic eye that elevates simple concepts into gallery-worthy work. First-timers appreciate her gentle approach and thorough aftercare guidance. Return clients trust her to build cohesive body collections that tell evolving stories.

Ben White Raven

Realism Master

Ben discovered his calling after apprenticing under Melbourne's realism pioneers for 3 years before opening his own practice. 10+ years later, his technical skill places him among Australia's top realism artists. He approaches every tattoo as permanent sculpture—understanding how skin moves, ages, and how ink settles to create lasting photographic quality.

Specializing in portrait work, wildlife realism, and vintage photo reproductions, Ben works almost exclusively on large-scale pieces that demand 10-40+ hours in the chair. His client list includes memorial portraits that bring tears, pet tributes that capture spirit, and family photographs that preserve moments forever. If you're ready to commit to a masterpiece, Ben's your artist.

Monique Moore

Bold Blackwork

Monique entered tattooing through punk culture and graphic design, bringing an outsider perspective that challenges traditional boundaries. 7+ years later, she's become Melbourne's go-to artist for bold blackwork, geometric patterns, and illustrative pieces that make statements. Her philosophy: tattoos should be fearless.

From solid black sleeves to intricate dotwork mandalas, ornamental designs to dark illustrative work—Monique specializes in high-contrast pieces with maximum visual impact. She works best with clients who know they want something bold, who trust her artistic vision, and who aren't afraid of the chair time required for large-scale coverage. Her work isn't for everyone. It's for those who go all in.
